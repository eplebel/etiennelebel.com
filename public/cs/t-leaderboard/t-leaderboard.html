Notes. Transparency audit of each author's latest 10 (or more) empirical published (peer-reviewed) papers (post-2010).

Transparency Requirements
  • (Open Access) = Working link to HTML/PDF full-text of article at journal or author's (or any co-author's) website (version of record, copy-edited, no pre-prints, without having to login (so Academia.edu or ResearchGate doesn't meet requirement).
  • (Open Data) = Working link to minimum dataset (for all studies) publicly posted online (personal websites allowed), no databook required, proprietary files allowed (e.g., SPSS .sav files, STATA files, etc.), and valid exemptions allowed (e.g., protected access login, ethical/confidential reasons).
  • (Basic Reporting) = Basic reporting of both (1) all competing interests/COIs *and* (2) funding sources in article or Curate Scholar author page.
  • Curate Science minimum Transparency standard is met when *ALL 3* requirements are met (which is a scaled back version of Curate Science's official "starter" transparency standard).
